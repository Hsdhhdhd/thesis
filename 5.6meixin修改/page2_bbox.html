Faculty of Engineering Technology Campus GROUP T Leuven — Master’s Thesis submitted to obtain the degree of Master of Science in Electronics and ICT Engineering Technology Academic Year 2025–2026 Pelvic Rotation Monitoring During Prolonged Sitting Design and Preliminary Evaluation of an IMU-Based Wearable Haptic Feedback System Luo Ruyang, Yuan Yonggui Master in Electronics and ICT , Faculty of Engineering Technology, Campus GROUP T Leuven, Andreas Vesaliusstraat 13, 3000 Leuven, Belgium Promotor(s): Prof. Bart Vanrumste Electronics and ICT , Faculty of Engineering Technology, Campus GROUP T Leuven, Andreas Vesaliusstraat 13, 3000 Leuven, Belgium, < bart.vanrumste@kuleuven.be > Co-promotor(s): Prof. Jean-Marie Aerts, Meixing Liao KU Leuven, Department of Biosystems, M3-BIORES, Kasteelpark Arenberg 30, 3001 Leuven, Belgium, < jean-marie.aerts@kuleuven.be > KU Leuven, Group T Leuven Campus, Andreas Vesaliusstraat 13, 3000 Leuven, Belgium, < meixing.liao@kuleuven.be > ABSTRACT This thesis develops and evaluates the technical feasibility of a wearable inertial measurement unit (IMU)-based haptic feedback system for monitoring pelvic rotation during prolonged sitting. The proto- type combines a Seeed Studio XIAO nRF54L15 Sense worn near the sacrum, a DRV2605L driver, and a coin vibration motor. An Android application records labelled IMU data over Bluetooth Low Energy, while a PC tool supports inspection. In trend-level comparison with a Shimmer reference, the XIAO reached Pearson correlations of 0.816–0.923 in a co-located test and field maximum cross-correlations up to 0.981 after lag correction. A five-participant pilot field study collected instructed, labelled seated segments from train journeys. In a single held-out-participant split, k-nearest neighbours (KNN) with k = 7 classified 2 s windows as static sitting or pelvic rotation with an F1-score of 0.957, detecting 155 of 165 pelvic-rotation windows and producing 4 false positives among 3,497 static windows. A com- pact deployable prototype-based KNN (12 features, 128 KMeans prototypes) reached an F1-score of 0.959 on the same held-out participant and was implemented on the wearable. Each 2 s classification cycle required 2.73 ms on-device, corresponding to compute time below 0.15% of the window period, with 56.3 KB of flash and 9.3 KB of RAM used. The firmware implements a 10 min inactivity timer that triggers a 1 s haptic reminder. These results support feasibility for the instructed pilot task, but they do not yet establish spontaneous daily-use robustness, battery life, or behavioural effectiveness of the reminder.
