## Non-verbatim Abstract Summary

This biomechanics study evaluates how sitting posture affects loads at cervico-thoracic and lumbosacral joints. It links posture variation to mechanical loading patterns in the spine and related joints.

## Relevance to This Thesis

Supports the posture and musculoskeletal rationale for monitoring sitting position.
