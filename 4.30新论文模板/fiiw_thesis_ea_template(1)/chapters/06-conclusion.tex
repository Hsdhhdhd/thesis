\chapter{Conclusion}\label{ch:conclusion}

This thesis built and tested a small wearable research prototype for detecting pelvic movement during prolonged sitting and providing haptic feedback when that movement is absent. The final prototype combines a XIAO nRF54L15 Sense sensor node worn near the sacrum, a DRV2605L-driven coin vibration motor, a BLE data stream, an Android application for labelled field recording, and a PC tool for researcher-side monitoring. The evaluation methods and results were separated to clarify the evidence chain: a co-located Shimmer reference comparison, a pilot field study in which five participants wore the device during a real urban route, a field-worn Shimmer/XIAO trend comparison from the same pilot outing, and a labelled train-sitting detection study. The final compact model was deployed on the target microcontroller.

The thesis therefore demonstrates the full technical chain required for a local wearable reminder: sensing, logging, annotation, trend-level comparison with a reference sensor, feature extraction, classifier comparison, prototype compression, embedded inference, and haptic actuation. This is broader than an offline classification experiment, but narrower than a behavioural intervention trial or finished engineering product.

That boundary is part of the contribution. The prototype shows that a pelvis-worn device can collect usable data, classify the target movement locally, and deliver a tactile cue without relying on cloud processing. It does not yet show that such cues change long-term sitting behaviour, improve comfort, or reduce health risk. Separating those claims keeps the technical findings useful without overstating their practical effect.

For RQ1, the embedded IMU captures motion patterns sufficiently consistent with a research-grade Shimmer reference for this detection task: co-located Pearson correlations were 0.816--0.923 with lag within 20~ms, and pilot field-worn maximum cross-correlations reached up to 0.981 after lag correction. This is technical, not clinical, validation, and only two of the five participants produced valid paired Shimmer recordings during the pilot field study.

For RQ2, the wearable platform supported real-time data collection and annotation in realistic transport contexts: all five participants completed the field route while wearing the device, and the Android application was used to label activities during the sessions. The weak point was hardware reliability rather than the software workflow, especially the intermittent field-recording gaps linked to the prototype battery-contact arrangement.

For RQ3, in the person-independent split (P1--P4 training, P5 testing), the \(k=7\) KNN classifier achieved an F1-score of 0.957 on pelvic rotation, with 155 of 165 pelvic-rotation windows detected and only 4 of 3{,}497 static-sitting windows misclassified. The compact 12-feature, 128-prototype version reached F1 = 0.959 on the same held-out participant and runs in 2.73~ms per 2~s window on the XIAO nRF54L15 Sense, corresponding to compute time below 0.15\% of the window period, with a flash footprint of 56.3~KB and a RAM footprint of 9.3~KB. The detector is therefore compatible with local deployment on the target device and sufficient for this preliminary instructed-movement dataset.

This work should be read as a feasibility and preliminary validation study rather than a behavioural intervention trial. It provides a working prototype pipeline that combines wearable hardware, mobile and PC software, field data, a Shimmer comparison, a deployable classifier, and measured on-device timing. The main unresolved issues are generalisation beyond instructed pelvic rotation, mechanical reliability during longer wear, battery life under continuous operation, and user response to repeated haptic reminders. A future study with more participants, more natural seated movement, more reliable hardware, and a dedicated user study of the haptic reminders can build on this pipeline.

Overall, the thesis shows that local pelvic-motion detection and cue delivery are technically feasible on a compact wearable platform. Its main value is to turn a concept for interrupting prolonged static sitting into a measurable prototype whose remaining weaknesses are now specific enough to guide the next iteration.
