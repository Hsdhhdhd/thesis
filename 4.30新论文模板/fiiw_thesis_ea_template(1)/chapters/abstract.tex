Many activities in modern daily life involve prolonged sitting, such as desk work, study, and commuting. However, prolonged sitting with insufficient postural variation may contribute to musculoskeletal discomfort, and pelvic rotation is clinically relevant to prevent and rehabilitate these discomforts. Existing studies largely focus on monitoring sitting duration or postures, while few closed the loop by providing real-time feedback based on monitoring outcomes, nor validated in continuous free-living settings. To address this gap, this thesis develops and evaluates the technical feasibility of an inertial measurement unit-based wearable prototype for monitoring prolonged sitting and providing real-time on-device haptic feedback to prompt movement. The prototype combines a Seeed Studio XIAO nRF54L15 Sense worn near the sacrum, a DRV2605L driver, and a coin vibration motor. An Android application records labelled IMU data over Bluetooth Low Energy, while a PC tool supports inspection. In trend-level comparison with Shimmer, a research-grade reference, the XIAO reached Pearson correlations of 0.816--0.923 in a co-located test and field maximum cross-correlations up to 0.981 after lag correction. To evaluate the prototype across varied seated contexts, a five-participant pilot field study was conducted in offices, trains, and buses, with instructed and subconscious pelvic rotations performed during prolonged sitting bouts. Using leave-one-subject-out cross-validation, k-nearest neighbours (KNN) with \(k=7\) classified 2~s windows as static sitting or pelvic rotation with an F1-score of 0.957, detecting 155 of 165 pelvic-rotation windows and producing 4 false positives among 3{,}497 static windows. A compact deployable prototype-based KNN (12 features, 128 KMeans prototypes) reached an F1-score of 0.959 on the same held-out participant and was implemented on the wearable. Each 2~s classification cycle required 2.73~ms on-device, corresponding to compute time below 0.15\% of the window period, with 56.3~KB of flash and 9.3~KB of RAM used. The firmware implements a 10~min inactivity timer that triggers a 1~s haptic reminder. Overall, the findings suggest that it is feasible to use a low-cost, lightweight, and low-power wearable system to detect instructed pelvic rotation during prolonged sitting and provide real-time haptic feedback without relying on continuous smartphone or PC-based processing. This pilot study supports the broader potential of unobtrusive wearable feedback systems for sedentary behavior intervention in varied sitting contexts in real world.

\underline{Keywords:} embedded machine learning, haptic feedback, inertial measurement unit, pelvic rotation, prolonged sitting, wearable sensing.
